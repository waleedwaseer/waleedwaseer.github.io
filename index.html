About

Dr. Waleed I. Waseer is a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), where he investigates nonlinear dynamics of Hopfions in chiral magnets and Kagome Spin Ice systems.

His doctoral research at COMSATS University Islamabad proposed a novel class of waves — Ghost Waves — exhibiting lossy behavior within lossless media, a fundamental contribution to electromagnetic theory.

He combines deep theoretical work in wave propagation and anisotropic materials with applied AI-driven inverse modeling for metasurface design, bridging classical electromagnetics and modern machine learning.

Research Areas

〰️

Electromagnetics & Wave Propagation

Ghost waves in anisotropic media, exceptional surface waves, non-uniform plane waves, Goos–Hänchen and Imbert–Fedorov shifts.

🔬

Metasurfaces & Metamaterials

Metamaterial-based sensors for chemical and biosensing, MXene solar absorbers, epsilon-near-zero slabs, graphene-coated structures.

🧲

Spintronics & Magnetic Systems

Hopfion dynamics in chiral magnets, Kagome Spin Ice, spin wave propagation, topological solitons, magnon interactions.

🤖

AI-Driven Electromagnetic Design

Deep neural network inverse modeling for metasurface absorbers, multi-channel spectral refinement, reducing FDTD simulation reliance.

📡

RF & Microwave Systems

Waveguide modeling, transmission line analysis, near-field simulations, signal processing for integrated photonic sensing.

⚙️

Computational Modelling

FDTD, FEM, COMSOL Multiphysics (RF Module), MuMax3, Python (PyTorch), MATLAB for simulation and data analysis.
