About

Dr. Waleed I. Waseer is a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), where his current work centers on machine learning for magnonics and nanophotonics — using deep neural networks to model and accelerate the design of complex physical systems that are otherwise prohibitively expensive to simulate.

His recent research applies deep neural networks to predict the optical response of MXene-based metasurface solar absorbers, and cyclic-consistent inverse design frameworks to map desired optical responses back onto nanophotonic structures — replacing thousands of FDTD/FEM sweeps with a trained surrogate model. In parallel, he studies the nonlinear dynamics of magnetic Hopfions in chiral ferromagnets and Kagome Spin Ice, using MuMax3 micromagnetic simulation alongside AI-driven inverse models to understand and engineer topological spin textures for frequency-multiplication and magnonic computing applications.

This applied AI work is grounded in a doctoral background in classical electromagnetic wave theory from COMSATS University Islamabad, giving his machine-learning models a physics-informed foundation rather than treating them as black boxes. Prior to UESTC, he held a Research Associate position at COMSATS and a visiting Research Scholar appointment at Penn State, and taught Electricity & Magnetism and Engineering Mathematics as a Lecturer at Quaid-i-Azam University.

02

Research

Replacing costly micromagnetic and full-wave simulation with trained AI surrogates — applied across topological spin textures and nanophotonic metasurfaces.

Surrogate Modeling

AI-Driven Inverse Design

Deep neural networks — GANs, DenseNet, and cyclic-consistent forward/inverse frameworks — trained as surrogate models that replace costly FDTD/FEM simulation sweeps, mapping a desired optical or magnetic response directly onto structural or material parameters.

Topological Spin Textures

Magnonics & Spintronics

Nonlinear dynamics of Hopfions in chiral ferromagnets and Kagome Spin Ice, studied via MuMax3 micromagnetic simulation — including harmonic generation, frequency multiplication, and magnon-mode excitation and control for magnonic-computing applications.

Metasurface Design

Nanophotonics & Metasurfaces

MXene-based solar absorbers and epsilon-near-zero slabs, with deep-learning models trained for spectral prediction and refinement, accelerating metasurface absorber design far beyond what repeated full-wave sweeps allow.

Physics-Informed Foundation

Computational Modelling

MuMax3 (GPU micromagnetics), COMSOL Multiphysics, CST Studio Suite, and FDTD/FEM methods, paired with Python (PyTorch) and MATLAB — grounding every machine-learning model in classical electromagnetic and micromagnetic theory rather than treating it as a black box.

Current Work & Goals

Extending cyclic surrogate-assisted frameworks from static metasurface design toward dynamic, time-dependent magnonic systems — using the same forward/inverse network pairing that accelerates optical metasurface design to accelerate the search for Hopfion and spin-ice configurations with targeted nonlinear response. The broader goal is a shared AI toolkit that treats magnon and photon design problems as the same class of inverse problem, cutting simulation cost across both domains.
